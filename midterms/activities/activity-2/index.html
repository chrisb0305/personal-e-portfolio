Activity 2: Operators

Christopher Balana | BSIT 2-5


This activity highlights the use of different Java operators—bitwise, ternary, assignment, arithmetic, relational, logical, and unary—to generate specific outputs through proper computation and syntax. It reinforces how operators control program flow, manipulate values, and evaluate conditions, ensuring that students understand both the mechanics and the logic behind operator usage in Java.


From my perspective, the task felt straightforward and manageable. The instructions were clear, and applying the operators came naturally since the concepts were already familiar. It was more of a practice exercise than a challenge, making it a smooth and confidence‑boosting activity.
